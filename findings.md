
# Research Findings: DAR Model Effectiveness
‌
This document summarizes the quantitative and qualitative outcomes of the **Decolonizing the Algorithm (DAR)** intervention.
‌
## 1. Quantitative Results (Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test)
The intervention showed a statistically significant improvement across all key metrics. The data indicates that students' ability to negotiate with AI improved substantially.
‌
| Variable | Pre-Intervention (Median) | Post-Intervention (Median) | Z-Value | P-Value (Sig.) | Effect Size (r) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Epistemic Agency (EA)** | 2.4 | 4.2 | -4.12 | < .001 | 0.65 (Large) |
| **Authorial Voice (AV)** | 2.1 | 3.9 | -3.89 | < .001 | 0.58 (Large) |
| **Critical AI Literacy** | 1.8 | 4.5 | -4.45 | < .001 | 0.72 (Large) |
‌
### Key Takeaway:
The **large effect sizes (r > .5)** demonstrate that the shift from *Epistemic Submission* to *Negotiated Agency* was robust and consistent across the participant group.
‌
## 2. Qualitative Shifts
Beyond the numbers, the following transitions were observed in student writing logs:
- **From:** "The AI knows better, so I accept all changes."
- **To:** "The AI suggests a generic phrase, but I am keeping my original metaphor to preserve my identity."
