
# Appendix: Guide to Resisting Prompts (DAR Model)
‌
In the DAR framework, "Resisting Prompts" are strategic instructions designed to prevent **Algorithmic Flattening** and maintain the author's **Epistemic Agency**.
‌
## 1. Preserving Cultural & Rhetorical Identity
**Goal:** To prevent the AI from removing non-Western rhetorical structures or cultural metaphors.
- *Standard Prompt:* "Fix the grammar in this paragraph."
- *DAR Resistant Prompt:* "Review this paragraph for grammatical clarity, but **do not alter the metaphorical expressions or the specific rhetorical sequence** I have used. I want to preserve my cultural voice while being linguistically accurate."
‌
## 2. Maintaining Argumentative Agency
**Goal:** To prevent the AI from "softening" or "hedging" a strong stance taken by the author.
- *Standard Prompt:* "Improve the tone of my argument."
- *DAR Resistant Prompt:* "Check the transitions in this argument, but **retain the directness and strength of my stance**. Do not introduce neutral hedging (e.g., 'it could be argued') if it weakens my original position."
‌
## 3. Critical Scrutiny of AI Suggestions
**Goal:** To force the AI to explain its changes, allowing the author to remain the final authority.
- *Standard Prompt:* "Rewrite this for me."
- *DAR Resistant Prompt:* "Suggest three different ways to rephrase this sentence, and **explain how each version changes the nuance of my authorial voice**. I will decide which one aligns best with my intended meaning."
